Chapter 1: Introduction

1.1 Background of the Study

The beauty industry in Somalia is experiencing rapid growth, with hundreds of new salons opening annually. Despite this growth, the management mechanisms remain largely manual. Many salon owners still use physical logbooks to record appointments, paper slips for financial transactions, and verbal communication for staff assignments.

This manual dependency leads to several critical issues, such as lost records, booking conflicts, and a total lack of historical data for business analysis. The "Salon Sanctuary" project was initiated to provide a localized, digital solution that bridges this gap between traditional service and modern management.

1.2 Problem Statement

Current inefficiencies in manual salon management:

✖ Record Fragmentation: Data is scattered across multiple physical notebooks.
✖ Inaccurate Accounting: Revenue and expenses are rarely reconciled daily.
✖ No Customer Loyalty: Without a digital database, tracking repeat customers is impossible.
✖ Operational Delays: Waiting for phone confirmations and manual schedule checks slows down service.

1.3 Objectives

The primary aim is to create a secure, high-performance web application for salon owners. Objectives include:

⚙️

Automation

Automate 90% of the booking and checkout workflow to allow staff to focus on services.

☁️

Cloud Access

Ensure that owners can monitor their salon's performance from any device, anywhere in the world.

📈

Data Insight

Provide daily, weekly, and monthly financial summaries through interactive charts.

1.4 Scope & Limitations

The scope includes Admin, Staff, and Cashier dashboards. It covers Appointments, POS, Financial tracking, Service management, and Client registration. A limitation is the requirement of an active internet connection for real-time cloud synchronization.

Chapter 2: Literature Review

2.1 Evolution of Business Management

Modern management theory suggests that "what cannot be measured, cannot be managed". The shift from manual to digital management in the service industry has proven to increase customer retention by 25% by allowing businesses to personalize services based on previous visit history.

2.2 Review of Related Works

We analyzed several existing salon management frameworks. While platforms like Booksy and Phorest offer extensive features, their complexity and pricing models are often a barrier for local Somali salons. Salon Sanctuary aims to provide a "lean" but powerful alternative tailored specifically for the local market.

Chapter 3: System Analysis & Design

3.1 Requirements Gathering

✔ Functional: Real-time booking, POS Checkout, Receipt Printing, Financial Reporting, Multi-role Support.
✔ Non-Functional: High security (TLS/SSL), Responsive UI, Somali Language Support, 99.9% Uptime.

3.2 Database Model (ERD Logic)

The system is designed with a core relational model. The bookings table acts as the central hub, linking customers, services, and staff together.

3.3 Design Principles

We followed a "Mobile-First for Cashiers" and "Data-First for Admins" philosophy. The dashboard is designed to be highly readable even on small laptop screens, using contrasting colors and large interactive elements.
